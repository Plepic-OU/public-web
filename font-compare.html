Font Options for Plepic Hero Heading

Brand: Playful, Experimental, Confidently Humble. Warm, organic, nature-digital. Not generic, not flashy, not cold. The font should feel like someone who knows their craft but doesn't need to prove it.

1. Crete Round

Current

Why: Warm slab serif with rounded terminals. Sturdy but friendly. Risk: Only has regular weight — no bold option, so headings feel thin. Limited expressiveness at display sizes.

Slab Serif

Why: Designed for comfortable reading on screen. Slab serif with personality — the serifs give it structure (digital/technology) while the warm curves keep it approachable. Has bold weights. Fits: "Confident humility" — sturdy without being loud.

3. Zilla Slab

Slab Serif

Why: Mozilla's open-source slab. Geometric precision with warmth — the slab serifs say "built/constructed" (transformation) while staying readable and friendly. Has full weight range. Fits: "Nature-digital fusion" — organic proportions, engineered details.

4. Vollkorn

Old Style

Why: Name means "whole grain" in German. The most organic option — calligraphic DNA, warm textures in the letterforms, slightly irregular curves. Feels handmade. Fits: "Curious play" — playful without trying, like a well-worn tool. Risk: Might read as too bookish/traditional.

Calligraphic

Why: Calligraphic serif with beautiful curves — the brushstroke heritage shows in the letter shapes. Warm and elegant at display sizes. Variable font. Fits: Wild Robot's handcrafted quality translated to type. Risk: Might feel too editorial/magazine-like.

6. DM Serif Display

High Contrast

Why: High contrast between thick and thin strokes creates drama at display sizes. Elegant and confident. The thick/thin contrast mirrors the transformation theme — something evolving. Fits: "Epic growth" — feels aspirational. Risk: Only one weight. Might feel too premium/luxury.

Variable
