1. The plepic.com website

What we collect

When you visit plepic.com we collect:

  • Usage analytics via Google Analytics 4 (property G-65CCEV6RS9): pages viewed, approximate country and city, device and browser, session duration, referring source. IP addresses are anonymised before storage.
  • Advertising measurement via Google Ads (conversion tag AW-17874572217): clicks from our ads and whether they led to a form submission, calendar booking, or contact action.
  • Contact events you trigger deliberately: clicking an email address, phone number, calendar link, or external form.

We do not run forms or collect account registrations on plepic.com. All signups happen through linked external services (Google Forms, Google Calendar), which have their own privacy policies.

Cookies

We set cookies only for analytics and ads measurement (_ga, _gid, _gcl_au). No advertising profile is built for you on our side; Google manages its own cookie lifecycles.

Legal basis

Legitimate interest (Article 6(1)(f) GDPR) in measuring and improving our own site and advertising. For visitors in the EU, we respect browser-level signals like Do Not Track and do not combine analytics identifiers with any personal profile held elsewhere.

2. YouTube automation tooling

Plepic operates the YouTube channel @plepic-agentic. We build and run an internal pipeline (the "YouTube Pipeline") that helps us publish, edit metadata on, and analyse performance of videos we produce ourselves. The pipeline is run only by Plepic's channel owner on a personal workstation.

YouTube API scopes we request

  • yt-analytics.readonly — read aggregated channel performance metrics for our own channel.
  • youtube.readonly — read channel metadata and list of our own videos.
  • youtube.upload — upload new videos to our own channel.
  • youtube.force-ssl — edit metadata (title, description, thumbnail, chapters) and manage comments on our own videos.

We request only the scopes strictly needed for channel owner operations. We never request access to other YouTube users' data.

How we handle YouTube data

  • OAuth refresh credentials are stored as an environment value on the operator's workstation (file permission 600, owner-only). They are not transmitted to any third-party service.
  • Channel and video performance data pulled via the API is stored as local JSON reports in our internal repository. We use it to inform our own content decisions.
  • We do not share data retrieved through YouTube APIs with any third party. We do not use it for advertising, profiling, or resale.
  • If you revoke access through myaccount.google.com/permissions, our tooling loses all access immediately and any cached data becomes stale.

Compliance with YouTube API Services

Our use of YouTube APIs complies with the YouTube API Services Terms of Service and Google's Privacy Policy. Data obtained through YouTube APIs is not shared, sold, or transferred to third parties, used for independent analysis outside the stated purposes, or used to build user profiles.

3. Email and CRM

When you email us, book a meeting, or engage in a sales conversation, we store your message and basic contact details in Google Workspace (Gmail, Google Calendar) and Pipedrive CRM to respond and maintain the relationship. We do not use this data for targeted advertising.

4. Your GDPR rights

You have the right to:

  • Object to processing, or restrict it

To exercise any right, email kaido@plepic.com. We respond within 30 days.

5. Retention

Analytics data is retained according to GA4's default 14-month window. Email and CRM records are retained for as long as the relationship is active and for up to 7 years afterwards to meet Estonian accounting-law retention requirements. Local YouTube analytics JSON reports are retained in our internal repository indefinitely for trend analysis, and contain no third-party personal data.
